Executive Summary

Mountain A/V represents a strategic expansion of Cloudcroft Reader and Mountain Monthly's journalism into audio-visual storytelling. This monthly video podcast will deepen community engagement, create new revenue opportunities, and extend the reach of local journalism in the Cloudcroft area.

Core Format: 30-minute monthly episodes featuring:

  • 10-minute co-host discussion (Hannah Dean & Johnny Rodriguez) analyzing the month's top stories
  • 10-20 minute guest interview with community leaders, newsmakers, and local voices

Launch Target: January 2026 with pilot episode featuring newly elected Mayor Dusty Wy

First-Year Budget: $4,200 (12 episodes @ $350/episode production fee)

I. Strategic Objectives

Primary Goals

1. Deepen Community Engagement

Create intimate, long-form conversations that written journalism cannot provide. Research shows that engaged journalism, where newsrooms build relationships with their audience by responding to community concerns, strengthens newsroom-community relationships and can provide needed revenue.[1] Build stronger connections between readers and local leaders, and establish a new platform for community dialogue during a period of significant village political transition.

2. Expand Audience Reach

  • Attract podcast listeners who may not currently read either publication
  • Leverage video format for social media distribution (YouTube, Facebook, Instagram Reels)
  • Create shareable content that introduces new audiences to Reader/Monthly journalism

3. Diversify Revenue Streams

Diversified revenue streams are essential for sustainability, with multiple sources including advertising/sponsorships, reader support (memberships/donations), events, and affiliate marketing.[2] For podcasts with smaller audiences, flat-rate local sponsorships and affiliate marketing often work better than CPM-based advertising.[3]

  • Develop sponsorship opportunities specific to audio/video format
  • Create premium content that demonstrates value for potential donors/members
  • Build foundation for future monetization as audience grows

4. Enhance Journalistic Impact

  • Provide platform for accountability journalism in accessible format
  • Document community history and leadership transitions
  • Complement written coverage with personality-driven storytelling

Success Metrics (Year One)

  • Engagement: 200+ views/listens per episode by month 6
  • Growth: 15% monthly audience increase
  • Community Impact: 80% of episodes generate follow-up community discussion or engagement
  • Revenue: Secure 1-2 local sponsors by month 6 ($100-200/episode)
  • Cross-promotion: 25% of podcast audience converts to Reader email subscribers

II. Format & Production Plan

Episode Structure

Total Runtime: 25-30 minutes

  1. Cold Open (0:30-1:00)
    • Compelling soundbite from guest or teaser of episode topic
    • Music/theme introduction
  2. Community News Segment (8-12 minutes)
    • Hannah and Johnny discuss 2-3 significant stories from the month
    • Analysis and context beyond written articles
    • "What we're watching" for upcoming stories
    • Conversational, not scripted - authentic voices
  3. Feature Interview (15-20 minutes)
    • Guest introduction with context
    • 3-5 prepared questions with room for organic follow-up
    • Focus on personality, process, and perspective (not just facts)
    • Opportunity for guests to speak directly to community
  4. Closing (1-2 minutes)
    • Preview next month's episode
    • Call to action (subscribe to Reader, support Monthly)
    • Contact information for listener questions

Production Workflow

Batch processing episodes (recording multiple at once) and grouping similar tasks together significantly improves efficiency.[4] The production workflow should include planning, recording, editing, mixing, sound design, and mastering.[5]

Pre-Production (Week 1-2)

  • Guest identification and booking (Hannah/Johnny collaboration)
  • Question development based on current news cycle
  • Story selection for discussion segment
  • Pre-interview research

Production (Week 3)

  • Recording session at Johnny's studio
  • Estimated recording time: 45-60 minutes for 30-minute episode
  • Batch recording option: Record 2-3 episodes in one session for efficiency

Post-Production (Week 3-4)

  • Editing and mixing (Johnny)
  • Creation of promotional assets:
    • 30-60 second teaser clips for social media
    • Audiogram for podcast platforms
    • Thumbnail image
    • Show notes with links and timestamps
  • Upload to hosting platform

Distribution & Promotion (Week 4)

  • Publish to podcast platforms (Apple Podcasts, Spotify, etc.)
  • Upload full video to YouTube
  • Create 3-5 social media clips
  • Email announcement to Reader subscribers
  • Embed in Mountain Monthly website
  • Reference in print edition

Technical Setup

Equipment (Johnny provides):

  • Video cameras and lighting
  • Quality microphones for host and guest
  • Audio recording/mixing equipment
  • Studio space at Johnny's residence

Software/Platforms Needed:

  • Audio editing software (Hindenburg Journalist recommended for news podcasts)
  • Video editing capability
  • Podcast hosting platform (recommendations: Buzzsprout, Libsyn, or Captivate)
  • YouTube channel for Mountain A/V
  • Social media management tools

Accessibility Requirements:

  • Full transcript of each episode (can use AI transcription services like Otter.ai, then edit)
  • Closed captions for video version
  • Show notes with time-stamped key moments

Growth Tactics (Based on Best Practices)

Small shows with highly committed fan bases consistently outperform larger shows with passive audiences in monetization metrics.[6]

  1. Leverage Existing Audience
    • Promote heavily to Reader's 3,000 subscribers in first 3 months
    • Print QR codes in Monthly directing to podcast
    • Social media campaign on established platforms
  2. Guest Cross-Promotion
    • Ask guests to share episode with their networks
    • Provide shareable graphics and clips
    • Tag guests in social media posts
  3. SEO Optimization
    • Detailed show notes with local keywords
    • Transcripts published on website
    • Episode titles optimized for search
  4. Community Events
    • Live podcast recording at local venue (quarterly)
    • Invite audience to submit questions
    • Create in-person connection to build loyalty
  5. Collaboration
    • Guest exchange with other New Mexico journalism podcasts
    • Regional podcast collaborations
    • Cross-promotion with compatible shows

Engagement Metrics to Track

  • Downloads/views per episode
  • Completion rate (how much of episode people listen to)

  • Social media engagement (shares, comments)
  • Website traffic from podcast

VII. Risk Mitigation & Contingency Planning

Identified Risks & Solutions

1. Budget Constraints

Risk: $4,200/year may strain publication budgets

Mitigation:

  • Start with quarterly episodes instead of monthly ($1,050/year)
  • Seek grant funding before launch commitment
  • Split costs between both publications
  • Build sponsorship pipeline early

2. Audience Size

Risk: Small initial audience may not justify investment

Mitigation:

  • Focus on engagement quality over quantity
  • Leverage existing 3,000+ subscriber base
  • Create shareable clips for viral potential
  • Patience - podcast growth is typically slow and steady

3. Production Sustainability

Risk: Johnny's availability after January uncertain (travel, other projects)

Mitigation:

  • Batch record multiple episodes when possible
  • Build 2-3 episode buffer
  • Consider seasonal format (3-month seasons vs. year-round)
  • Develop backup production plan

4. Guest Booking

Risk: Difficulty securing high-quality guests monthly

Mitigation:

  • Book 2-3 months ahead
  • Maintain rolling guest list
  • Consider occasional "just Hannah & Johnny" episodes
  • Expand guest criteria beyond elected officials

5. Technical Issues

Risk: Equipment failure, platform problems

Mitigation:

  • Always record backup audio
  • Test equipment before each session
  • Have contingency recording location
  • Build extra time into production schedule

Alternative Format Options

If monthly proves unsustainable:

Option A: Seasonal Model

  • 3-4 episodes per quarter, released weekly
  • 3-month production, 3-month break pattern
  • Allows for planning and resource concentration

Option B: Quarterly Deep Dives

  • 4 episodes per year, 45-60 minutes each
  • Major topics only (budget, election, annual review, special investigations)
  • Lower total cost, higher impact per episode

Option C: Video Newsletter

  • 5-10 minute monthly video recap
  • No guests, just Hannah/Johnny discussion
  • Lower production cost, easier to sustain
  • Can add guests when opportunities arise

XI. Conclusion

Mountain A/V represents a strategic opportunity to deepen the Cloudcroft Reader and Mountain Monthly's community impact during a pivotal moment in village politics and leadership. By combining professional journalism with accessible audio-visual storytelling, this podcast can:

  • Strengthen connections between residents and local leaders
  • Create new revenue streams for local journalism
  • Expand audience reach beyond current readers
  • Document community history and civic discourse
  • Demonstrate innovative sustainability models for rural news

The timing is right: New mayor, engaged community, existing audience base, and production capability all align.

The risk is manageable: Starting small with pilot episode, exploring grant funding, and building sponsorship pipeline provide multiple paths to sustainability.

The opportunity is significant: As local news deserts expand across rural America, Mountain A/V can demonstrate that small-town journalism can innovate, adapt, and thrive.

With careful planning, diversified funding, and commitment to community service, Mountain A/V can become a valued institution in Cloudcroft's civic life and a model for rural journalism innovation.
